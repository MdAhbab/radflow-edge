# Pneumonia Recognition and Management (WHO/IMCI digest)

Source: WHO Integrated Management of Childhood Illness and adult ARI guidance
Source-ID: WHO-IMCI-PNEU
Topic: pneumonia, consolidation, infiltration, air bronchogram, fast breathing

## Radiographic pattern
Lobar or patchy consolidation, air bronchograms, and focal infiltration support
pneumonia. Silhouette sign localises the lobe involved. New focal opacity in a
patient with fever and cough is presumptive pneumonia.

## Clinical severity classification
- Fast breathing thresholds (breaths/min): >=50 for infants 2-12 months,
  >=40 for children 1-5 years, >=30 for adults, indicate lower respiratory
  infection.
- Danger signs: inability to drink/feed, central cyanosis, severe chest
  indrawing, lethargy, or SpO2 below 90-92%.

## Triage actions at a primary health centre
- Uncomplicated pneumonia in a stable patient: start oral amoxicillin first-line
  and arrange review in 48 hours.
- Provide supportive care: antipyretics, fluids, and counselling on return
  precautions.

## When to escalate urgently
Any danger sign, SpO2 below 92%, respiratory rate above 30 in an adult, or
suspected sepsis warrants urgent referral for oxygen and parenteral antibiotics.
