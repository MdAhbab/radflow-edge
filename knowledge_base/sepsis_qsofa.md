# Sepsis Recognition and qSOFA (digest)

Source: Surviving Sepsis Campaign and Sepsis-3 consensus
Source-ID: SEPSIS-QSOFA
Topic: sepsis, qSOFA, septic shock, deterioration, early warning

## qSOFA quick bedside score (one point each)
- Respiratory rate >= 22 breaths per minute.
- Altered mentation (Glasgow Coma Scale below 15 / new confusion).
- Systolic blood pressure <= 100 mmHg.

A qSOFA score of 2 or 3 in a patient with suspected infection identifies high risk
of poor outcome and should prompt urgent escalation and assessment for organ
dysfunction.

## Supporting signs of sepsis
Fever or hypothermia, tachycardia, hypoxia (SpO2 below 92%), reduced urine
output, mottled skin, and lactate elevation where measurable. A normal qSOFA does
not exclude sepsis; clinical judgement overrides the score.

## Triage actions at a primary health centre
- Apply the sepsis "first hour" actions: oxygen to keep SpO2 at or above 94%,
  intravenous fluids for hypotension, blood cultures if available, and early
  empiric antibiotics.
- Reassess vitals frequently; sepsis can deteriorate within minutes.

## When to escalate urgently
qSOFA >= 2, SpO2 below 92%, systolic BP <= 100 mmHg with suspected infection, or
new confusion warrants immediate referral to a facility with resuscitation
capacity.
