# Normal Chest Radiograph and No-Acute-Finding Guidance (digest)

Source: Primary-care radiology triage guidance
Source-ID: NORMAL-CXR
Topic: normal, no acute finding, reassurance, return precautions

## Interpretation
A radiograph reported as no acute cardiopulmonary finding does not exclude
disease. Early pneumonia, small nodules, and many non-radiographic conditions can
present with a normal film. The image is one input; symptoms and vitals govern
disposition.

## Triage actions at a primary health centre
- When the detector finds no significant abnormality and vitals are normal,
  manage symptomatically and give clear return precautions.
- If symptoms are significant despite a normal film (persistent cough beyond two
  weeks, weight loss, hemoptysis), pursue the TB diagnostic pathway regardless of
  the radiograph.
- Document the negative read and the safety-netting advice given.

## When to escalate
Persisting or worsening symptoms, any danger sign, or abnormal vitals warrant
clinician review even with a normal radiograph. A normal image must never
override an abnormal patient.
