# Cardiomegaly and Heart Failure on Chest Radiograph (digest)

Source: ESC Heart Failure guidance, adapted for primary-care triage
Source-ID: HF-TRIAGE
Topic: cardiomegaly, enlarged heart, pulmonary edema, effusion, heart failure

## Radiographic pattern
Cardiothoracic ratio above 0.5 on a PA film suggests cardiomegaly. Supporting
signs of decompensation include upper-lobe vascular redistribution, Kerley B
lines, perihilar "bat-wing" alveolar edema, and small bilateral pleural
effusions.

## Clinical correlation
Cardiomegaly is a structural finding, not a diagnosis. Correlate with dyspnea,
orthopnea, paroxysmal nocturnal dyspnea, raised jugular venous pressure, and
peripheral edema. Hypertension, ischemic heart disease, and valvular disease are
common underlying causes.

## Triage actions at a primary health centre
- Record blood pressure, heart rate, and SpO2. Assess for acute pulmonary edema
  (severe breathlessness, frothy sputum, hypoxia).
- For stable chronic findings, arrange non-urgent clinician review with ECG and,
  where available, echocardiography.

## When to escalate urgently
Acute pulmonary edema, SpO2 below 92%, systolic BP below 90 or a hypertensive
emergency, or chest pain warrants urgent referral.
